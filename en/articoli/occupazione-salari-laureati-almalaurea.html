AlmaLaurea 2025

Which university should you choose?

A data-driven reading of AlmaLaurea 2025 data on employment and wages of Italian graduates. Comparing universities, disciplinary groups and course types helps distinguish paths that lead more quickly to work, paths that pay more and paths that take longer to stabilise.

Source: AlmaLaurea 2025Observed cohorts: 2024 and 2020Processed by Nazareno Lecis

The analysis uses the AlmaLaurea 2025 survey. Graduates observed one year after graduation belong to the 2024 cohort. Graduates observed five years after graduation belong to the 2020 cohort. The employment definition used is the less restrictive one, which also includes paid training activities.

The files used to build the charts are available in the analisi_almalaurea repository. The figures in the article are embedded views from the interactive AlmaLaurea dashboard or outputs built from the same JSON published on the site.

What the AlmaLaurea 2025 data sheets cover

The 2025 survey involved almost 700,000 first- and second-cycle graduates from 78 Italian universities participating in AlmaLaurea. The main block covers 320,000 graduates from 2024 observed one year after graduation. The survey also includes 129,000 second-cycle graduates from 2022 observed after three years and 125,000 second-cycle graduates from 2020 observed after five years.

The five-year charts should be read within the second-cycle perimeter. In the online data sheets used here, the documentation at three and five years concerns second-cycle graduates. The one-year total also includes first-cycle graduates, so the two totals are not directly comparable without filtering by course type.

How to read the data

The scatterplots show each disciplinary group as a point. The horizontal axis shows net monthly salary. The vertical axis shows the employment rate. Bubble size indicates the number of observed graduates.

The values are aggregated. A disciplinary group point does not represent an individual person. It represents the observed average for that group after applying filters for cohort, years since graduation, employment definition and course type.

One year after graduation, differences are already large

One year after graduation, the picture is very heterogeneous. Across all courses, net monthly salary ranges from EUR 1,022 for Sports and Exercise Sciences to EUR 1,672 for Medical, Health and Pharmaceutical. ICT reaches EUR 1,608, and Industrial and Information Engineering EUR 1,635.

The difference also concerns the employment rate. Psychology records the lowest value, at 47.3 percent. Medical, Health and Pharmaceutical reaches 86.7 percent. Education and Training is at 76.2 percent. ICT is at 71.9 percent.

Bachelor degrees have a weaker transition

Among first-cycle degrees, the median of average salaries by disciplinary group is EUR 1,144. The median employment rate across disciplinary groups is 47.4 percent. Some groups have particularly low values. Scientific has an employment rate of 29.4 percent. Humanities and Literature is at 33.7 percent. Psychology is at 36.1 percent.

The most likely explanation is institutional. In many fields, the bachelor degree is not treated as a terminal qualification. A substantial share of graduates continues to a master degree. The one-year figure therefore measures an intermediate stage of the educational path.

Two-year master degrees show better outcomes already after one year

Two-year master degrees show a stronger transition. One year after graduation, the median of average salaries by disciplinary group is EUR 1,415 and the median employment rate is 83.6 percent. ICT reaches EUR 1,771 and 93.3 percent employment. Industrial and Information Engineering reaches EUR 1,787 and 93.6 percent.

ICT and engineering combine high demand, transferable skills and greater immediate employability in the private sector. Medical-health and pharmaceutical paths have very high employment rates because they include professionalising paths and health professions with structural demand.

Following the same cohort

The comparison between the 2024 cohort after one year and the 2020 cohort after five years is useful for reading two moments in the path, but it does not follow the same graduates. To follow a cohort, the graduation year must be fixed. The 2020 cohort, for example, can be observed after one year in the 2021 survey and after five years in the 2025 survey.

After five years, employment is high, but the perimeter is different

Five years after graduation, the picture changes. Employment rates are much higher and more concentrated. The minimum value among disciplinary groups is 88.1 percent for Humanities and Literature. The maximum is 97.5 percent for Industrial and Information Engineering.

These five-year data must be read within the perimeter of second-cycle graduates. They are not the direct continuation of the total one-year chart, because the one-year total also includes first-cycle degrees.

Wages remain very different even after five years

Five years after graduation, within the second-cycle perimeter, net monthly salary ranges from EUR 1,477 for Education and Training to EUR 2,226 for ICT.

How to use these data to choose

These data should not replace preferences, abilities, family constraints, the place where one wants to live and personal interest. They make explicit part of the information that students often receive late or in a confusing way.

Choosing a university should include at least three questions. What are the average outcomes of the path. How much the result varies between universities and territories. How terminal the course is or how often it requires a later level.

Conclusion

The data describe associations, not causal effects. They do not make it possible to say how much of the result depends on the degree course, the university, the territory, student selection, the sector of entry or individual characteristics.

The most prudent reading is this. In Italy, the economic value of a degree varies greatly by disciplinary area, course type and phase of the post-graduation path. After one year, bachelor-degree rates can be low because many graduates are continuing their studies. After five years, the standard perimeter of the data sheets concerns the second cycle. Within this perimeter, employment is high almost everywhere, while wage differences remain large.
